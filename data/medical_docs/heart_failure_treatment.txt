Heart Failure Treatment Overview

Definition:
Heart failure is a clinical syndrome resulting from structural or
functional impairment of ventricular filling or ejection of blood.

Classification (NYHA Functional Class):
- Class I: No limitation of physical activity
- Class II: Slight limitation, comfortable at rest
- Class III: Marked limitation, comfortable only at rest
- Class IV: Unable to carry out any activity without symptoms

Ejection Fraction Categories:
- HFrEF: Heart failure with reduced EF (< 40%)
- HFpEF: Heart failure with preserved EF (>= 50%)
- HFmrEF: Heart failure with mildly reduced EF (41-49%)

Pharmacological Treatment (HFrEF):

1. ACE Inhibitors/ARBs/ARNI
   - First-line for all HFrEF patients
   - ARNI (Sacubitril/Valsartan) preferred over ACE inhibitor
   - Reduces mortality and hospitalizations

2. Beta-Blockers (Carvedilol, Metoprolol, Bisoprolol)
   - Initiated when patient is stable
   - Reduces heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand
   - Proven mortality benefit

3. Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists (Spironolactone)
   - Added for NYHA Class II-IV
   - Reduces mortality and hospitalizations
   - Monitor potassium closely

4. SGLT2 Inhibitors (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin)
   - New standard of care
   - Benefits in diabetes and non-diabetes
   - Reduces cardiovascular death

5. Loop Diuretics (Furosemide)
   - Symptom management (fluid overload)
   - No mortality benefit
   - Dose adjusted based on volume status

Monitoring:
- Daily weight monitoring
- Electrolytes and renal function every 1-3 months
- Echocardiography annually
- BNP/NT-proBNP levels
