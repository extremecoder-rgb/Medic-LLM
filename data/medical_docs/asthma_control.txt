Asthma Management and Control

Definition:
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disorder of the airways characterized
by reversible airflow obstruction, bronchial hyperresponsiveness,
and underlying inflammation.

Symptoms:
- Wheezing
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness
- Coughing (especially at night or early morning)

Classification by Severity:
- Intermittent: Symptoms <= 2 days/week, nighttime <= 2x/month
- Mild Persistent: Symptoms > 2 days/week, nighttime 3-4x/month
- Moderate Persistent: Daily symptoms, nighttime > 1x/week
- Severe Persistent: Symptoms throughout day, frequent nighttime

Treatment:

Step 1 (Intermittent):
- Short-acting beta-agonist (Albuterol) as needed

Step 2 (Mild Persistent):
- Low-dose inhaled corticosteroid (ICS)
- Alternatives: Leukotriene modifier

Step 3 (Moderate Persistent):
- Low-dose ICS + Long-acting beta-agonist (LABA)
- Or medium-dose ICS

Step 4 (Moderate-Severe):
- Medium-dose ICS + LABA
- Consider adding LAMA (Tiotropium)

Step 5 (Severe):
- High-dose ICS + LABA
- Consider biologic therapy (Omalizumab, Mepolizumab)
- Or oral corticosteroids

Inhaler Technique:
- Shake inhaler before use
- Breathe out fully
- Activate inhaler while breathing in slowly
- Hold breath for 10 seconds
- Rinse mouth after ICS use

Asthma Action Plan:
- Green Zone: All clear, continue medications
- Yellow Zone: Caution, increase treatment
- Red Zone: Medical emergency, seek immediate care

Triggers to Avoid:
- Tobacco smoke
- Dust mites
- Pet dander
- Pollen
- Cold air
- Air pollution
- Respiratory infections
