Type 2 Diabetes Management Summary

Overview:
Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by
hyperglycemia resulting from insulin resistance and relative insulin deficiency.
It accounts for approximately 90-95% of all diabetes cases.

Risk Factors:
- Obesity (BMI > 30)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Family history of diabetes
- Age > 45 years
- History of gestational diabetes

Diagnosis:
- Fasting plasma glucose >= 126 mg/dL
- HbA1c >= 6.5%
- Random plasma glucose >= 200 mg/dL with symptoms
- Oral glucose tolerance test >= 200 mg/dL

Treatment Goals:
- HbA1c target: < 7% for most adults
- Blood pressure: < 130/80 mmHg
- LDL cholesterol: < 100 mg/dL
- Weight management: 5-10% weight loss if overweight

First-Line Medication:
- Metformin 500mg twice daily, titrate to 1000mg twice daily
- Reduces hepatic glucose production
- Improves insulin sensitivity
- Weight neutral

Monitoring:
- HbA1c every 3-6 months
- Annual renal function (eGFR, urine albumin)
- Annual dilated eye examination
- Annual foot examination
- Regular blood pressure monitoring

Lifestyle Modifications:
- Medical nutrition therapy
- Regular physical activity (150 min/week moderate intensity)
- Weight management
- Smoking cessation
