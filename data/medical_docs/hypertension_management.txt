Hypertension Management Guidelines

Definition:
Hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure >= 130 mmHg
or diastolic blood pressure >= 80 mmHg on repeated measurements.

Classification:
- Normal: < 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129/< 80 mmHg
- Stage 1: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2: >= 140/>= 90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: > 180/> 120 mmHg

Complications:
- Coronary artery disease
- Heart failure
- Stroke
- Chronic kidney disease
- Retinopathy
- Peripheral vascular disease

Pharmacological Treatment:

First-Line Agents:
1. ACE Inhibitors (Lisinopril, Enalapril)
   - Block angiotensin-converting enzyme
   - Renoprotective in diabetes
   - Monitor: potassium, creatinine

2. ARBs (Losartan, Valsartan)
   - Alternative if ACE inhibitor cough occurs
   - Similar benefits to ACE inhibitors

3. Calcium Channel Blockers (Amlodipine)
   - Vasodilation without reflex tachycardia
   - Effective in elderly patients

4. Thiazide Diuretics (Hydrochlorothiazide)
   - Reduce blood volume
   - Effective in salt-sensitive hypertension

Treatment Targets:
- General population: < 130/80 mmHg
- Diabetes with CKD: < 130/80 mmHg
- Elderly (>65): < 130 systolic if tolerated

Lifestyle Modifications:
- DASH diet (rich in fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy)
- Sodium restriction (< 2300 mg/day)
- Regular aerobic exercise
- Weight loss (1 kg loss = 1 mmHg reduction)
- Limit alcohol intake
- Smoking cessation
